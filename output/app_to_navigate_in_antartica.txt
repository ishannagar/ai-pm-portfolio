Problem Statement
Navigating in Antarctica presents unique challenges due to extreme weather, lack of infrastructure, limited GPS reliability near the poles, and life-threatening conditions. Current navigation tools are not optimized for Antarctic-specific hazards like crevasses, whiteouts, and rapidly changing ice conditions. Users need a specialized navigation solution that works offline and accounts for polar environment factors.
------------------------------------------------------------
Target Users
- Research scientists and field teams conducting expeditions
- Tour operators and expedition guides leading groups
- Logistics coordinators managing supply routes and base operations
------------------------------------------------------------
User Stories (3)
- As a field researcher, I want offline maps with marked hazards and safe routes, so that I can navigate safely when satellite connectivity fails.
- As an expedition guide, I want real-time weather overlays and visibility warnings, so that I can make informed decisions about route safety.
- As a logistics coordinator, I want to track team locations and share waypoints, so that I can coordinate movements and respond to emergencies.
------------------------------------------------------------
Success Metrics
- Daily active users during Antarctic field season (Nov-Feb)
- Offline map download completion rate before expeditions
- Emergency SOS activations with successful coordinate transmission
- User-reported incidents avoided due to hazard warnings
- Route completion time vs. planned time (efficiency metric)
------------------------------------------------------------
Technical Considerations
- Offline-first architecture with pre-downloadable map tiles for all Antarctic regions
- Integrate magnetic declination corrections for polar navigation accuracy
- Support satellite communication APIs (Iridium, Inmarsat) for emergency messaging
- Battery optimization for extreme cold (minimize GPS polling, efficient data sync)
- Integrate weather data feeds (Antarctic Meteorological Research Center, NOAA)
- Implement coordinate sharing protocol compatible with rescue services
------------------------------------------------------------
Risks
- Device failure in extreme cold renders app unusable; mitigate with testing at -40°C and recommending ruggedized devices.
- Inaccurate hazard data leads to safety incidents; mitigate with crowd-sourced validation and partnerships with Antarctic research stations.
- Limited satellite bandwidth increases sync costs; mitigate with aggressive data compression and selective sync options.
- Regulatory restrictions on technology use in Antarctica; mitigate with early consultation with Antarctic Treaty organizations.
